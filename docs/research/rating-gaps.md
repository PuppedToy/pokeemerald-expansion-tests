| Fake Out | move | Fake Out appears on nearly every VGC champion/finalist team in the corpus (Incineroar in 2018/2019 winners, Mega Kangaskhan, Ludicolo, Hitmontop, Raichu, Smeargle). Its value is a guaranteed turn-1 flinch that buys a free setup/positioning turn (Belly Drum Snorlax, Geomancy Xerneas), not damage. In rating.js it is a 40-BP damage move scored by rateMove (~10*40/140 = 2.9) plus the +3 EFFECT_FIRST_TURN_ONLY bump = ~5.9, and it is NOT in DOUBLES_SUPPORT_RATINGS, so rateMoveDoubles gives it no doubles floor. | Add MOVE_FAKE_OUT to DOUBLES_SUPPORT_RATINGS with a floor around 7.5-8 (peer to Follow Me/Tailwind). It is arguably the single most ubiquitous doubles support move and is currently under-rated in the exact format it dominates. |
| Snarl / Fake Tears (spread special-attack drop) | move | Spread SpA-drop support is a defining doubles win condition: Incineroar/Arcanine Snarl weaken both foes' special damage (Paul Ruiz's 2018 title and the AFK core are built on it), and Fake Tears enables special sweeps. In rateMove a spread stat-drop on the *target* is treated as a downside (hasSpaDrop -> rating *= 0.9), and Snarl gets only the generic spread damage multiplier (1.35x on ~55 BP ~= 5.3). Neither Snarl nor Fake Tears has a DOUBLES_SUPPORT_RATINGS floor. | In the doubles path, treat a spread offensive stat-drop as a bonus rather than a penalty, and give MOVE_SNARL / MOVE_FAKE_TEARS / MOVE_STRUGGLE_BUG a doubles floor ~6-7. The category-suppression they provide is a primary strategy, not chip. |
| Icy Wind / Electroweb / Bulldoze / Rock Tomb (spread speed control) | move | Persistent, item-free spread speed control is an entire doubles axis parallel to Tailwind/Trick Room: Cresselia and A-Ninetales Icy Wind, Tapu Koko Electroweb (Mudhiman Top-4 NAIC), Latias Icy Wind, Landorus Rock Tomb. These are low-BP moves (~55/35) so the damage rater scores them low, and rateMove even applies hasSpeDrop -> *= 0.95. The spread speed drop that IS the point is penalized, and none has a doubles floor. | Give spread Speed-drop moves a doubles floor (~5.5-6, below Tailwind's 7.5 since it is weaker but repeatable) and remove/invert the speed-drop penalty when the move is a spread move in doubles. |
| Entry hazards in doubles (Stealth Rock, Spikes, Toxic Spikes, Sticky Web) | move | The rater rates Stealth Rock 8, Sticky Web 8, Toxic Spikes 6.5, Spikes 6 in statusList, and rateMoveDoubles passes these through unchanged (no spread, no doubles floor entry) so they keep their high singles score. In doubles/VGC (bring 6 / pick 4, far fewer switches) hazards are near-worthless and almost never seen in the corpus of 4-mon doubles teams; they are a singles-only value. This is an OVER-valuation for the doubles format. | In rateMoveDoubles, down-weight hazard-setting moves (e.g. multiply hazard ratings by ~0.35-0.5, or cap at ~3). Hazards are the clearest 'great in singles, dead in doubles' case. |
| Priority-granting abilities (Prankster, Gale Wings, Triage) | ability | These abilities are not in DOUBLES_ABILITY_RATINGS and get only their base data rating, yet they define multiple corpus teams. Prankster gives priority to Tailwind/Taunt/Thunder Wave (Thundurus, Whimsicott's terrain Z-Nature Power, Tornadus Tailwind) = decisive tempo. Gale Wings makes Brave Bird a +1 priority 'win button' (Talonflame across 2014-2016 teams). A naive rater sees no stat/damage effect and rates them near zero. | Add Prankster / Gale Wings / Triage as doubles ability floors (~6-7) and, ideally, a per-mon combo bonus in computeComboBonus when the mon carries the moves these enable (Prankster + Tailwind/Taunt/T-Wave; Gale Wings + Brave Bird; Triage + drain move). |
| Taunt | move | Taunt is rated a flat 5 in statusList regardless of user speed or Prankster. In the corpus fast/Prankster Taunt is a premier disruption tool: Krookodile's anti-setup Taunt was singled out as key to Ryota Otsubo's 2017 Worlds win; Mega Gengar Taunt shut off Geomancy/Trick Room/Tailwind for Wolfe Glick; Thundurus/Tornadus/Mandibuzz/Whimsicott all weaponize it. 5 undersells a move that neutralizes whole strategies (Trick Room teams, redirection, screens, recovery stall). | Raise the base Taunt rating to ~6.5 and add a bonus when the user is fast or has Prankster (priority Taunt). Consider a doubles floor since Taunt gates the format's setup/speed-control archetypes. |
| Aurora Veil | move | MOVE_AURORA_VEIL sits in the top '0' group of statusList (rated 0), while Light Screen and Reflect are 7.5 each. Aurora Veil is both screens in one move and was the engine of Sam Pandelis's 2017 Worlds runner-up 'veil offense' and a defining defensive-setup archetype. Its only condition is snow/hail, and the rater already tracks hasAbility('SNOW_WARNING') context. | Rate Aurora Veil at least as high as a single screen (~7.5), and higher (~9) when the mon or a plausible teammate has Snow Warning. Rating it 0 is almost certainly a straightforward bug/oversight. |
| Perish Song + trapping abilities (Shadow Tag, Arena Trap, Magnet Pull) | ability | Perish Song is rated 2 in statusList and the trapping abilities are absent from DOUBLES_ABILITY_RATINGS. Together they form Perish Trap, Emilio Forbes's 2018 Worlds runner-up plan (Mega Gengar Shadow Tag + double Perish Song), and Shadow Tag also enabled Se Jun Park's Gothitelle and Wolfe Glick's Gengar to delete answers. A naive rater sees no damage/stat effect for either piece. | Raise Perish Song to ~5-6 and give it a large combo bonus when the mon (or team) has a trapping ability or Mean Look/Block; add Shadow Tag/Arena Trap/Magnet Pull as doubles ability floors (~6-7) reflecting the trap-and-remove utility. |
| Terrain-setting abilities (Electric/Grassy/Misty/Psychic Surge) and terrain moves | ability | The Tapus' Surge abilities are not in DOUBLES_ABILITY_RATINGS and terrain moves are rated a flat 2 in statusList, yet terrain defines the entire Gen-7 corpus (Tapu Koko/Fini/Lele/Bulu are among the most-used mons; Kokolucha HO, Ash-Greninja balance, Tapu Lele priority-block, terrain-Z Whimsicott). Terrain grants team-wide STAB boosts, status/priority immunity, and enables items (Seeds) and Unburden sweeps. | Add Electric/Grassy/Misty/Psychic Surge as ability floors (~6-7, relevant in singles too), lift terrain moves above 2, and add combo bonuses for terrain + Seed items / Unburden (Hawlucha) and Psychic Terrain + priority-vulnerable setup. |
| Speed over-reward vs slow bulky attackers under Trick Room | role | Trick Room already gets a doubles floor of 7, but the deeper gap is that Trick Room INVERTS the value of Speed: under it, low Speed with high bulk/power is optimal. The corpus is full of deliberately slow win conditions (Snorlax, Stakataka, Torkoal, Cresselia, Bronzong, Primal Groudon-Adamant, Xerneas-Bold) built for TR. A rater that always rewards high Speed will systematically under-rate slow bulky attackers and over-rate raw Speed for these builds. | When a team/build carries Trick Room (or when scoring a very slow, bulky, high-Attack mon), stop penalizing low Speed and add a 'TR-abuser' bonus. Treat Speed as double-edged rather than monotonic good. |
| Weather-abuser roles dependent on a teammate setter (Swift Swim, Chlorophyll, Sand Rush, Slush Rush) | role | computeComboBonus boosts damage when a mon sets its OWN weather (DRIZZLE/DROUGHT handling), but the corpus's rain/sun cores split the setter and the abuser: Politoed(Drizzle) + Ludicolo/Swampert(Swift Swim), Torkoal(Drought) + Lilligant(Chlorophyll). A per-mon rater sees Swift Swim/Chlorophyll do nothing on their own and under-rates these sweepers, and cannot credit the setter for enabling a partner. | Add team-level weather-pairing awareness: if the team has a weather setter, treat matching Swift Swim/Chlorophyll/Sand Rush/Slush Rush mons as having doubled Speed (a large bonus). Absent that, keep them low. This is a team-composition signal the per-mon rater currently misses. |
| Regenerator | ability | Regenerator (33% heal on switch) is the backbone of the corpus's balance/pivot cores (Toxapex, Tornadus-T, Amoonguss, Slowbro, and the AFK core), making pivots effectively un-wearable and rewarding U-turn/Volt Switch loops. It has no combat-stat effect so a damage-focused rater assigns it little value. | Give Regenerator a meaningful ability floor (~6-7) and/or a combo bonus when paired with pivoting moves (U-turn/Volt Switch/Flip Turn/Teleport) or a defensive role, since its value is sustained switching, not a stat. |
| Unaware | ability | Unaware (ignore the foe's stat boosts) is the stall answer to setup sweepers and a recurring win condition/wall in the corpus (Clefable across ORAS/USUM stall teams). It has no offensive footprint, so a naive rater rates it near zero, missing that it single-handedly counters the boosting archetypes (Calm Mind, Swords Dance, Quiver Dance) that the same corpus shows winning games. | Give Unaware a solid rating on bulky mons with recovery (~6-7). It is a defensive counter-strategy, valuable in inverse proportion to how much the rater rewards setup moves elsewhere. |
| Contrary + self-lowering STAB (Leaf Storm, Overheat, Draco Meteor, Superpower) | ability | rating.js line ~773 has an explicit unresolved '// Drops for contrary' note, and selfLoweringMoves are treated only via white-herb handling. With Contrary, Leaf Storm/Overheat/Draco Meteor/Superpower become +1/+2 boosting nukes (Serperior's Leaf Storm is a snowballing sweep in the ORAS corpus). The rater currently treats these moves' self-drops as a cost even for Contrary users. | When the ability is Contrary, invert self-lowering moves from a penalty into a boost (treat Leaf Storm/Overheat/etc. as strong repeatable setup-plus-damage). Resolve the flagged TODO in computeComboBonus. |
| Disguise / guaranteed-free-turn abilities | ability | Mimikyu's Disguise guarantees a free turn to land Trick Room, Taunt, Destiny Bond, or setup (Nils Dunlop and Mudhiman both used it as a Trick Room / disruption 'panic button'). Functionally it is a built-in Substitute for one hit, but a damage/stat-focused rater sees only a typing gimmick. | Give Disguise (and similar free-turn enablers) a bonus, especially when the mon carries a high-value one-shot utility move (Trick Room, Taunt, Destiny Bond, a setup move) whose landing it guarantees. |
| Spread self-hitting moves negated by teammate immunities (Earthquake, Surf, Heat Wave) | move | rateMoveDoubles gives ally-hitting spread moves (FOES_AND_ALLY / ALL_BATTLERS, e.g. Earthquake/Surf) a reduced 1.2x vs 1.35x for foes-only spread, to price in friendly fire. But the corpus routinely negates that friendly fire with Flying/Levitate/Ground-immune/Storm Drain/Telepathy partners (Gardevoir Telepathy immune to partner EQ, Lando/Salamence Flying, Rotom/Bronzong Levitate, Gastrodon Storm Drain), making spread EQ a premier move. The rater has no way to grant the full multiplier when the team removes the drawback. | When the team/partner is immune to the spread type (Flying/Levitate/Ground-immunity/Telepathy/matching absorb ability), treat ally-hitting spread moves as foes-only (full 1.35x). At minimum, flag Earthquake/Surf as high-value on teams built around Ground/Flying immunities. |
| Bulky-offense item valuation (Assault Vest, Weakness Policy, Focus Sash on leads, Choice lock) | item | Corpus item choices reward the opposite of naive heuristics: Assault Vest makes all-attacking bulky mons un-KOable (Kartana, Tapu Koko, Incineroar, Snorlax-class, Raichu, Buzzwole) despite 'losing status moves'; Weakness Policy turns a bulky mon that WANTS to be hit into a +2/+2 sweep (Taida's Aegislash); Focus Sash on frail suicide leads guarantees a hazards/redirection/Dark Void turn (Smeargle, Greninja, Terrakion). A naive item rater tends to penalize 'no status moves' (AV), 'needs to be hit' (WP), and Sash on non-frail mons. | In rateItemForAPokemon, reward Assault Vest on movesets that are all-damage + bulky, reward Weakness Policy on bulky setup mons with a super-effective-bait typing, and reward Focus Sash specifically on fast fragile support/lead roles rather than treating these as generic/negative. |
