### Mega Medicham Offense

- **Format:** Gen 6 ORAS OU (Smogon singles, 6v6)
- **Era:** Gen 6 ORAS (2014-2016) · **Event:** Smogon official ORAS OU Sample Teams (community resource) · **Author:** ABR & blunder · **Confidence:** high
- **Archetype:** Offense (wallbreaker spam / bulky offense)
- **Strategy:** Mega Medicham (Pure Power) is one of ORAS's hardest-hitting wallbreakers; it punches holes with Fake Out + High Jump Kick + elemental punches, then Choice Specs Keldeo and Life Orb Latios clean up the openings. Jirachi's Healing Wish and Latios's Defog keep the frail breakers coming back healthy and hazard-free.

|---|---|---|---|---|
| Medicham-Mega | Pure Power | Medichamite | Fake Out, High Jump Kick, Thunder Punch, Ice Punch | Primary wallbreaker (Adamant, 252 Atk / 4 Def / 252 Spe) |
| Keldeo-Resolute | Justified | Choice Specs | Scald, Hydro Pump, Secret Sword, Hidden Power Bug | Special wallbreaker (Timid, 4 Def / 252 SpA / 252 Spe; source lists HP Bug / HP Flying as the 4th slot) |
| Landorus-Therian | Intimidate | Choice Scarf | Earthquake, Stone Edge, Knock Off, U-turn | Scarf revenge killer / pivot (Jolly, 72 HP / 240 Atk / 196 Spe) |
| Rotom-Wash | Levitate | Leftovers | Volt Switch, Hydro Pump, Will-O-Wisp, Thunder Wave | Defensive pivot / status spreader (Bold, 248 HP / 252 Def / 8 SpD) |
| Jirachi | Serene Grace | Leftovers | Stealth Rock, Iron Head, U-turn, Healing Wish | Stealth Rock + Healing Wish support (Careful, 240 HP / 236 SpD / 32 Spe) |
| Latios | Levitate | Life Orb | Draco Meteor, Hidden Power Fire, Defog, Recover | Special attacker + Defog hazard control (Timid, 252 SpA / 4 SpD / 252 Spe) |

**Synergies:**
- Healing Wish Jirachi fully restores a chipped Medicham or Keldeo, letting the wallbreakers return at full HP for a second break
- Medicham (Fighting/Psychic) + Keldeo (Water/Fighting) fighting-spam overwhelms shared answers while Latios pressures the bulky Psychics/Waters that wall them
- Latios Defog + Jirachi rocks + Rotom-W pivoting keep momentum and hazards in the team's favor for the frail attackers
- Scarf Landorus-T + Intimidate + U-turn provide revenge-killing and safe pivots into Medicham

**Anti-synergies:**
- Medicham and Keldeo share Fighting typing, so bulky Psychics/Fairies (Clefable, Slowbro, Mega Sableye, Latios) can wall the primary breakers at once
- Medicham is frail with no recovery and is momentum-dependent; if Latios/Defog is removed, hazards wear the offense down fast

### KeldTar Bulky Offense

- **Format:** Gen 6 ORAS OU (Smogon singles, 6v6)
- **Era:** Gen 6 ORAS (2014-2016) · **Event:** Smogon official ORAS OU Sample Teams (community resource) · **Author:** Leftiez & ABR · **Confidence:** high
- **Archetype:** Bulky Offense
- **Strategy:** The definitive ORAS 'KeldTar' core: Choice Specs Keldeo + Choice Band Tyranitar, where Tyranitar Pursuit-traps the Psychic/Ghost-types (Latios, Slowbro, Gengar) that would wall Keldeo, freeing Keldeo to spam its Water STAB. - Tyranitar Pursuit-traps Latios/Slowbro/Gengar/Psychics that wall Keldeo, the core KeldTar synergy that opens Keldeo's Water STAB

- Scarf Landorus-T revenge-kills and generates U-turn momentum into the breakers; Intimidate cushions physical hits

**Anti-synergies:**
- No dedicated hazard removal (Latios runs an offensive Surf set, not Defog), so Stealth Rock + Spikes can grind the team down; it relies on pressure and Scizor/Clefable recovery
- Two Choice-locked members (Specs Keldeo, Band Tyranitar) can be forced out and exploited by fast switches / bulky pivots

### Mega Sableye Stall (The Great Walls)

- **Format:** Gen 6 ORAS OU (Smogon singles, 6v6)
- **Era:** Gen 6 ORAS (2014-2016) · **Event:** Smogon ORAS OU RMT thread (posted May 2015) · **Author:** SnipingxLlama · **Confidence:** medium
- **Archetype:** Full Stall
- **Strategy:** Mega Sableye's Magic Bounce reflects entry hazards, Taunt and status back at the opponent, denying the chip damage stall normally fears. The wall core (Chansey, Slowbro, Jirachi, Tentacruel, Clefable) then grinds foes down with Will-O-Wisp, Toxic/Toxic Spikes and Seismic Toss, sustained by Wish, Recover and Regenerator.

|---|---|---|---|---|

| Chansey | Natural Cure | Eviolite | Wish, Protect, Seismic Toss, Stealth Rock | Special wall + Wish passer + Stealth Rock (Bold, 252 HP / 252 Def / 4 SpD) |
| Slowbro | Regenerator | Leftovers | Slack Off, Scald, Psyshock, Toxic | Physical wall / Regenerator pivot (Bold, 252 HP / 252 Def / 4 SpA) - author explicitly flagged this as 'easily the most contested spot on the team' |
| Jirachi | Serene Grace | Leftovers | Fire Punch, Body Slam, Iron Head, Wish | Fairy/Steel check + paraflinch + Wish support (Impish, 252 HP / 224 Def / 32 Spe) |
| Tentacruel | Clear Body | Black Sludge | Rapid Spin, Scald, Acid Spray, Toxic Spikes | Special wall + hazard control (Rapid Spin) + Toxic Spikes (Calm, 224 HP / 252 SpD / 32 Spe) |

**Synergies:**
- Sableye Magic Bounce (bounces enemy hazards) + Tentacruel Rapid Spin/Toxic Spikes = two-way hazard control while stacking the team's own poison
- Chansey Wish + Jirachi Wish pass health to the frailer walls; Regenerator Slowbro and Recover keep the core topped up
- Clefable's Unaware ignores enemy stat boosts, covering the setup sweepers that Sableye and the blobs cannot outlast
- Will-O-Wisp (Sableye) + Toxic / Toxic Spikes (Slowbro/Tentacruel) status-stack to whittle down anything that switches in

**Anti-synergies:**
- Classic stall weakness to elite wallbreakers (Mega Medicham, Mega Heracross, Manaphy, Mega Gardevoir) and to Mold Breaker users (Excadrill) that set hazards straight past Magic Bounce
- Poison/Steel-types that ignore the Toxic-based win condition (e.g. opposing Ferrothorn) can wall the team; the author himself flagged the Slowbro slot as contested, indicating imperfect defensive coverage

### Ash-Greninja Balance

- **Format:** Gen 7 USM OU (Smogon Singles)
- **Era:** Gen 7 Sun/Moon & Ultra Sun/Ultra Moon (megas + Z-moves era) · **Event:** Smogon USM OU Sample Teams v2 (official resource) · **Author:** Finchinator · **Confidence:** high
- **Archetype:** Balance
- **Strategy:** A three-pivot defensive backbone (Regenerator Toxapex/Tornadus-T + bulky Celesteela) absorbs hits and pivots momentum into two elite wallbreakers, Choice Specs Ash-Greninja and Choice Band Tapu Bulu, while Rockium-Z Garchomp sets Stealth Rock and converts into a Swords Dance / Continental Crush sweeper late-game.

| Pokémon | Ability | Item | Moves | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Toxapex | Regenerator | Black Sludge | Toxic, Scald, Recover, Haze | Defensive pivot / status spreader / setup deterrent (Haze) |
| Celesteela | Beast Boost | Leftovers | Heavy Slam, Leech Seed, Protect, Flamethrower | Bulky wall & soft win condition (Leech Seed stall) |
| Greninja-Ash | Battle Bond | Choice Specs | Hydro Pump, Dark Pulse, Spikes, Water Shuriken | Primary special wallbreaker / revenge killer (transforms on KO, Water Shuriken) |
| Garchomp | Rough Skin | Rockium Z | Stealth Rock, Swords Dance, Earthquake, Stone Edge | Stealth Rock setter + Z-Swords Dance late-game sweeper (Continental Crush) |
| Tapu Bulu | Grassy Surge | Choice Band | Wood Hammer, Superpower, Stone Edge, Horn Leech | Immediate physical wallbreaker + Grassy Terrain support |
| Tornadus-Therian | Regenerator | Rocky Helmet | Hurricane, Knock Off, Defog, U-turn | Defog hazard control / Regenerator pivot |

**Synergies:**
- Three Regenerator/bulky pivots (Toxapex, Tornadus-T, Celesteela) form a defensive core that checks much of the tier and freely pivots breakers in
- Tapu Bulu's Grassy Terrain boosts Grass moves, passively heals grounded teammates, and halves Earthquake damage
- One-sided hazard game: Ash-Greninja & Garchomp stack Spikes + Stealth Rock while Tornadus-T Defogs away the opponent's
- VoltTurn-style momentum via U-turn (Torn-T) hands free entries to Specs Greninja and Band Bulu

**Anti-synergies:**
- Tornadus-T's Defog can accidentally clear Garchomp's own Stealth Rock if mistimed
- Choice-locking on Specs Greninja and Band Bulu makes them exploitable by faster/immune switch-ins
- Celesteela and Tornadus-T share an Electric weakness, and several members dislike strong Fire coverage

- Ferrothorn + Ash-Greninja stack Spikes; Tornadus-T Defog keeps the frail rain sweepers off hazards
- Ferrothorn covers the Water-stacked team's Grass/Electric/Dragon weaknesses

**Anti-synergies:**
- Hard weather timer (5/8 turns): once rain ends, Swift Swim Swampert and boosted Hydro Pumps fall off, and opposing sun/sand setters can override it
- Heavy Water-move stacking is walled by bulky Water/Grass answers (Ferrothorn, Toxapex, opposing Water absorbers)
- Only one true Grass resist (Ferrothorn) on an otherwise Water-heavy squad

### Mega Aggron Stall

- **Format:** Gen 7 USM OU (Smogon Singles)
- **Era:** Gen 7 Sun/Moon & Ultra Sun/Ultra Moon (megas + Z-moves era) · **Event:** Smogon USM OU Sample Teams v2 (official resource) · **Author:** Credited as "Bitchinator" in the USM OU Sample Teams v2 thread · **Confidence:** high
- **Archetype:** Stall
- **Strategy:** A textbook full-stall build: Mega Aggron (Filter, colossal physical bulk) and Eviolite Chansey split the physical/special defensive load into a near-unbreakable wall, then Stealth Rock + Toxic + Wish/Regenerator recovery grind the opponent down while Unaware Clefable neutralizes setup sweepers and double Defog keeps hazards off. | Chansey | Natural Cure | Eviolite | Seismic Toss, Soft-Boiled, Toxic, Heal Bell | Special wall + team cleric (Heal Bell) |
| Clefable | Unaware | Leftovers | Wish, Moonblast, Calm Mind, Protect | Unaware setup-sweeper answer / Wish support / win condition |
| Alomomola | Regenerator | Rocky Helmet | Wish, Toxic, Knock Off, Protect | Physical wall & Wish passer (Regenerator recovery) |
| Mew | Synchronize | Leftovers | Earthquake, Soft-Boiled, Will-O-Wisp, Defog | Utility pivot / Defog / Will-O-Wisp burns |

- Mega Aggron (physical, Filter) + Chansey (special) give an almost complete physical/special defensive split
- Alomomola Wish + Regenerator plus Chansey Soft-Boiled/Heal Bell create a team-wide passive recovery and status-cure engine
- Clefable's Unaware ignores opposing stat boosts - the stall answer to Calm Mind/Swords Dance sweepers
- Double Defog (Mew + Zapdos) keeps hazards off a team that depends on staying at high HP; Toxic + Stealth Rock provide the win condition

**Anti-synergies:**
- Very passive - strong wallbreakers, Taunt users, and Substitute/Magic Guard mons that dodge Toxic can overwhelm or PP-stall it
- Heavy reliance on Toxic means Poison/Steel/Magic-Guard foes wall the primary damage source
- Double Defog is somewhat redundant, and full stall struggles in mirror 'stall wars' and against strong Ground/Fighting breakers

### Ash-Greninja Balance

- **Format:** Gen 7 (SM/USUM) OU — Smogon singles, 6v6 (all six brought)
- **Era:** Gen 7 SM/USUM, ~2017-2019 · **Event:** Smogon official USM OU Sample Teams v2 resource (community-curated by top players) · **Author:** Finchinator · **Confidence:** high
- **Archetype:** Balance (Regenerator-pivot balance with hazard-stack + wallbreakers)
- **Strategy:** Layer Spikes (Ash-Greninja) plus Stealth Rock (Garchomp) and grind the opponent down through Regenerator pivots (Toxapex, Tornadus-T) while Choice Specs Ash-Greninja and Choice Band Tapu Bulu punch holes; Garchomp cleans late-game with Swords Dance + Rockium Z after checks are chipped or removed.

| Greninja-Ash | Battle Bond | Choice Specs | Hydro Pump, Dark Pulse, Spikes, Water Shuriken | Special wallbreaker and revenge killer; transforms on a KO, Water Shuriken gives boosted priority, also lays Spikes (documented set despite the unusual Choice Specs + Spikes pairing) |

- Regenerator core (Toxapex + Tornadus-T) enables sustainable pivoting and repeated switch-ins
- Spikes (Greninja) + Stealth Rock (Garchomp) chip lets the wallbreakers break through bulky walls over a few turns
- Grassy Terrain (Tapu Bulu) boosts its own Grass STAB, passively heals grounded teammates, and softens opposing Earthquakes
- Tornadus-T Defog keeps hazards off the team's own switch-heavy members

**Anti-synergies:**
- Grassy Terrain halves the power of Garchomp's own Earthquake against grounded targets, and heals opposing grounded Pokemon
- Toxapex is the only real special wall, so very strong special breakers can pressure the back
- Garchomp is the sole Stealth Rock setter — if it is pressured or trapped, hazard pressure collapses
- Choice-locked Tapu Bulu can be exploited for momentum once revealed

### Rain Offense (Pelipper + Mega Swampert + Ash-Greninja)

- **Format:** Gen 7 (SM/USUM) OU — Smogon singles, 6v6 (all six brought)
- **Era:** Gen 7 SM/USUM, ~2017-2019 · **Event:** Smogon official USM OU Sample Teams v2 resource (community-curated by top players) · **Author:** ABR & BKC · **Confidence:** high
- **Archetype:** Weather — Rain offense (Swift Swim + Water-spam)
- **Strategy:** Pelipper sets rain so Swift Swim Mega Swampert and Choice Specs Ash-Greninja fire off doubled-power, near-unresisted Water attacks at boosted speed, while rain also makes Tornadus-T's Hurricane 100% accurate; Magearna is a rain-independent setup sweeper as a secondary win condition.

| Pokémon | Ability | Item | Moves | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pelipper | Drizzle | Damp Rock | Scald, U-turn, Defog, Roost | Rain setter (Damp Rock extends to 8 turns), Defog support and slow U-turn pivot |
| Greninja-Ash | Battle Bond | Choice Specs | Water Shuriken, Dark Pulse, Surf, Ice Beam | Rain-boosted special breaker and revenge killer with Water Shuriken priority |
| Swampert-Mega | Swift Swim | Swampertite | Stealth Rock, Waterfall, Earthquake, Ice Punch | Swift Swim rain sweeper (doubled speed) and Stealth Rock setter |
| Ferrothorn | Iron Barbs | Leftovers | Leech Seed, Spikes, Power Whip, Knock Off | Hazard setter and defensive glue; walls opposing Water/Grass/Electric that threaten the rain mode |
| Tornadus-Therian | Regenerator | Life Orb | Taunt, Hurricane, Knock Off, Superpower | Rain-boosted (100% accurate Hurricane) special breaker; Taunt shuts down setup and hazards |
| Magearna | Soul-Heart | Steelium Z | Shift Gear, Calm Mind, Flash Cannon, Thunderbolt | Rain-independent Shift Gear/Calm Mind setup sweeper and Fairy/Steel check |

**Synergies:**
- Rain doubles Water damage, gives Mega Swampert doubled Swift Swim speed, and makes Tornadus-T Hurricane 100% accurate all at once
- Ferrothorn walls the opposing Water/Grass/Electric types that would otherwise flip the rain matchup
- Water-spam plus Steel/Flying/Fairy/Electric coverage across Greninja, Swampert, Tornadus-T and Magearna leaves very little that resists everything
- Magearna provides a weather-independent win condition if rain is unavailable

**Anti-synergies:**
- Rain-dependence: if Pelipper faints or the 8-turn rain lapses, Mega Swampert and Ash-Greninja lose their speed/power edge
- Stacking multiple Water-types compounds a shared Grass and Electric weakness
- Opposing Ferrothorn, Toxapex and bulky Grass-types wall the core Water spam
- Only one dedicated Defogger (Pelipper), which is also the irreplaceable rain setter

### Swords Dance Mega Mawile Bulky Offense (Sand)

- **Format:** Gen 7 (SM/USUM) OU — Smogon singles, 6v6 (all six brought)
- **Era:** Gen 7 SM/USUM, ~2017-2019 · **Event:** Smogon official USM OU Sample Teams v2 resource (community-curated by top players) · **Author:** Ske · **Confidence:** high
- **Archetype:** Bulky offense with Sand support (Tyranitar Sand Stream, double Intimidate)
- **Strategy:** Assault Vest Tyranitar sets sand and sponges special hits at the back while Swords Dance Mega Mawile (Huge Power Play Rough + Sucker Punch priority) wallbreaks; Choice Scarf Landorus-T, double Intimidate and Amoonguss's Spore keep the team resilient enough to set up and clean.

| Pokémon | Ability | Item | Moves | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tyranitar | Sand Stream | Assault Vest | Rock Slide, Crunch, Earthquake, Pursuit | Sand setter, Assault Vest special sponge, and Pursuit trapper for Psychics/Ghosts |
| Mawile-Mega | Intimidate | Mawilite | Play Rough, Swords Dance, Knock Off, Sucker Punch | Swords Dance wallbreaker/cleaner; Huge Power Play Rough plus Sucker Punch priority, Intimidate on switch |
| Landorus-Therian | Intimidate | Choice Scarf | Earthquake, Stone Edge, U-turn, Toxic | Choice Scarf revenge killer and U-turn pivot; second Intimidate |
| Heatran | Flash Fire | Firium Z | Magma Storm, Taunt, Stealth Rock, Earth Power | Stealth Rock setter, Magma Storm trapper and Taunt stallbreaker with a Firium Z nuke |
| Tornadus-Therian | Regenerator | Rocky Helmet | Hurricane, Knock Off, U-turn, Defog | Defog hazard control, Knock Off utility and Regenerator pivot glue |
| Amoonguss | Regenerator | Black Sludge | Giga Drain, Spore, Hidden Power Fire, Stun Spore | Spore/Stun Spore disruptor and Regenerator pivot; checks Water/Grass/Ground |

**Synergies:**
- Double Intimidate (Mega Mawile + Landorus-T) blunts physical attackers so the frailer breakers survive
- Amoonguss Spore and Stun Spore neutralize a threat or generate free setup turns for Mega Mawile
- Tyranitar Pursuit-traps the Psychics/Ghosts (Tapu Lele, Latios) that pressure the Steel-heavy core
- Heatran + Tyranitar form a strong special/Steel-Fire defensive backbone; Scarf Lando-T revenge-kills what breaks through

**Anti-synergies:**
- Sand here is incidental (bulk/Pursuit) rather than a true weather engine — there is no sand sweeper like Excadrill to exploit it
- Sand chip damages the team's own Tornadus-T and Amoonguss, partly working against their Regenerator recovery
- Heatran and Mega Mawile share a Ground weakness; fast special attackers are threatening once Tyranitar is gone
- The main breakers lack reliable recovery, so they wear down over a long game

### Speed-Boosting Blacephalon Hyper Offense

- **Format:** Gen 7 (USUM, pre-Zygarde-ban) OU — Smogon singles, 6v6 (all six brought)
- **Era:** Gen 7 USUM, before Zygarde's Gen 7 OU ban · **Event:** Smogon official USM OU Sample Teams v2 resource; dates to the USUM window before Zygarde's Gen 7 OU ban · **Author:** Xtra$hine · **Confidence:** high
- **Archetype:** Hyper offense (hazard-lead + stacked speed-boosting setup sweepers)
- **Strategy:** Lead with a Focus Sash suicide setter (Greninja or Excadrill) to lay Spikes + Stealth Rock and Taunt the opposing lead, then overwhelm with a stack of speed-boosting setup sweepers (Dragon Dance Zygarde, Dragon Dance Mega Gyarados, Shift Gear/Calm Mind Magearna, Calm Mind Blacephalon) that each cover one another's checks.

| Pokémon | Ability | Item | Moves | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Zygarde | Aura Break | Iapapa Berry | Dragon Dance, Thousand Arrows, Extreme Speed, Dragon Tail | Dragon Dance sweeper; Thousand Arrows hits Flying/levitators, Extreme Speed is priority. Source lists Aura Break (Power Construct was the more common competitive ability) and the 4th slot as Dragon Tail/Coil/Substitute. Zygarde was later banned from Gen 7 OU. |
| Greninja | Protean | Focus Sash | Spikes, Ice Beam, Hidden Power Fire, Taunt | Suicide Spikes lead; Focus Sash + Taunt guarantees hazards vs opposing leads |
| Magearna | Soul-Heart | Leftovers | Shift Gear, Calm Mind, Thunderbolt, Ice Beam | Dual-dance (Shift Gear + Calm Mind) setup sweeper and secondary win condition |
| Gyarados-Mega | Mold Breaker | Gyaradosite | Dragon Dance, Crunch, Earthquake, Substitute | Dragon Dance sweeper; Mold Breaker Earthquake ignores Levitate. Source gives the 3rd slot as Substitute/Taunt/Waterfall. |
| Blacephalon | Beast Boost | Ghostium Z | Calm Mind, Shadow Ball, Flamethrower, Substitute | Calm Mind + Substitute sweeper; Beast Boost snowballs Speed, Ghostium Z is the breaking nuke |
| Excadrill | Mold Breaker | Focus Sash | Stealth Rock, Rapid Spin, Earthquake, Toxic | Secondary Focus Sash lead — Stealth Rock setter and Rapid Spin hazard control |

**Synergies:**
- Every sweeper boosts its own Speed (Dragon Dance / Shift Gear / Beast Boost), so whichever survives can clean once its checks are gone
- Spikes (Greninja) + Stealth Rock (Excadrill) chip lets the sweepers break sashes and bulky answers
- Mold Breaker on Mega Gyarados and Excadrill ignores Levitate and other blocking abilities
- Zygarde's Extreme Speed provides priority to pick off faster or weakened threats

**Anti-synergies:**
- Two Focus Sash leads (Greninja + Excadrill) are partly redundant and both die to hazards, weather or multi-hit chip that breaks the Sash
- No defensive backbone at all — the team folds if it loses offensive momentum
- Stacked setup sweepers are all cold to fast priority, to phazing/Haze (e.g. Toxapex), and to Unaware walls like Clefable
- Relies on Zygarde as a centerpiece, which was subsequently banned from Gen 7 OU, dating the team to a narrow legal window
