### Follow Me — Se Jun Park's 2014 World Champion team

- **Format:** VGC 2014 (Gen 6 XY doubles; Kalos regional dex; bring 6 / pick 4, flat level 50)
- **Era:** Gen 6 (XY), megas era · **Event:** 2014 Pokemon World Championships, Masters Division — 1st place (Washington, D.C.) · **Author:** Se Jun Park (Sejun), South Korea · **Confidence:** high
- **Archetype:** Redirection & trapping support offense (bulky offense)
- **Strategy:** Weaken targets with Pachirisu (Super Fang chip + Nuzzle paralysis) while Follow Me redirects attacks, then clean up with priority Brave Bird Talonflame and a Dragon Dance Mega Gyarados sweep; Gothitelle's Shadow Tag traps and removes specific answers. Typical 4-mon core: Pachirisu + Mega Gyarados + Talonflame + Garchomp, with Gothitelle/Gardevoir flexed to the matchup. VERIFIED: the primary Nugget Bridge report matches every ability, item and moveset exactly (and lists natures/EVs); Park's 1st-place finish and roster are corroborated by the Worlds teams article and pokemon.com.

| Pachirisu | Volt Absorb | Sitrus Berry | Follow Me, Nuzzle, Super Fang, Protect | Redirection + paralysis support (the iconic pick) |
| Gothitelle | Shadow Tag | Leftovers | Psyshock, Thunder Wave, Tickle, Protect | Trapper / disruptor |
| Gardevoir | Telepathy | Choice Scarf | Dazzling Gleam, Psychic, Moonblast, Swagger | Fast special attacker / speed control |
| Garchomp | Rough Skin | Focus Sash | Earthquake, Dragon Claw, Rock Slide, Protect | Physical attacker / Kangaskhan check |
| Talonflame | Gale Wings | Life Orb | Brave Bird, Overheat, Taunt, Quick Guard | Priority sweeper / Quick Guard support |

**Synergies:**
- Pachirisu Follow Me redirects hits away from setup Gyarados; Volt Absorb makes Pachirisu immune to Electric, indirectly covering Gyarados's 4x Electric weakness
- Nuzzle paralysis gives the slower sweepers effective speed control
- Talonflame Quick Guard blocks priority (Fake Out, Sucker Punch, opposing Gale Wings) on Gyarados setup turns; Super Fang chip lets Brave Bird pick up KOs
- Gothitelle Shadow Tag traps and removes dedicated Gyarados answers
- Telepathy Gardevoir is immune to its own partner's spread Earthquake

**Anti-synergies:**
- Mega Gyarados is 4x weak to Electric — the whole plan leans on Pachirisu; remove Pachirisu and the sweepers are exposed
- Only one Mega, so the offense hinges on a fragile redirection/trap engine
- Swagger is luck-dependent (self-hit confusion risk)

### What's Protect? — Alex Ogloza's US Nationals Champion team

| Talonflame | Gale Wings | Choice Band | Brave Bird, Flare Blitz, U-turn, Taunt | Priority attacker / U-turn pivot |

**Anti-synergies:**
- No Protect on the whole team (Aegislash's King's Shield is the only protective move) — the team cannot scout, stall Trick Room, or dodge double-targeting, so it is very exposed to spread damage
- Rain weakens the team's own Fire coverage (Talonflame Flare Blitz)
- Heavy Choice-locking risks being walled or forced out; opposing weather (Drought/Sand) flips Ludicolo's Swift Swim advantage

### The Power of Pledge — Serapis's swamp-terrain offense

- **Format:** VGC 2014 (Gen 6 XY doubles; Kalos regional dex; bring 6 / pick 4, flat level 50)
- **Era:** Gen 6 (XY), megas era · **Event:** Philadelphia Regional Championships 2014 — made top cut (7-2 Swiss record; eliminated in the first elimination round) · **Author:** Jeremy Rodrigues (Serapis) · **Confidence:** medium
- **Archetype:** Terrain-based speed control offense (Pledge 'swamp' — non-weather, non-Trick-Room field control)
- **Strategy:** Lead Greninja + Mega Venusaur and fire Water Pledge + Grass Pledge together to create a swamp that halves all opposing Speed for 3 turns, then sweep with normally-slower attackers (Dragon Dance Haxorus, Specs Chandelure) that now outspeed. Well-documented with full sets, but a niche top-cut regional team rather than a championship staple. Typical 4-mon core: Greninja + Mega Venusaur + Haxorus + Chandelure. VERIFIED against the single primary Nugget Bridge report, which matches all sets and confirms the documented Zapdos item mistake; single-source and niche, hence medium confidence. Softened the original 'Top 32' claim to 'made top cut' as the exact cut size was not stated in the source.

| Pokémon | Ability | Item | Moves | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Greninja | Protean | Focus Sash | Water Pledge, Ice Beam, Taunt, Protect | Fast Pledge partner / disruptor |
| Venusaur (Mega) | Chlorophyll -> Thick Fat (as Mega) | Venusaurite | Grass Pledge, Sludge Bomb, Sleep Powder, Protect | Mega / Pledge partner / sweeper-disruptor |
| Chandelure | Infiltrator | Choice Specs | Heat Wave, Shadow Ball, Hidden Power Ice, Energy Ball | Special attacker (thrives under the swamp speed drop; ignores screens/Substitute) |
| Haxorus | Mold Breaker | Life Orb | Dragon Claw, Earthquake, Rock Slide, Dragon Dance | Dragon Dance physical sweeper / win condition under swamp |
| Lucario (Mega) | Inner Focus (Adaptability as Mega) | Lucarionite | Aura Sphere, Flash Cannon, Quick Guard, Protect | Alternate Mega / special attacker / Quick Guard support |

**Synergies:**
- Water Pledge (Greninja) + Grass Pledge (Venusaur) fired together = ~160 BP Grass hit plus a swamp that lowers all opposing Speed 50% for 3 turns — artificial speed control without weather or Trick Room
- Under the swamp, Life Orb Dragon Dance Haxorus and Choice Specs Chandelure suddenly outspeed and sweep; Infiltrator Chandelure ignores screens and Substitute
- Venusaur Sleep Powder neutralizes setup threats like Mega Mawile; Lucario Quick Guard blocks priority (e.g. Sucker Punch) on vulnerable setup turns

**Anti-synergies:**
- Two Mega Stones (Venusaurite + Lucarionite) but only one Mega per battle — Mega Lucario and Mega Venusaur compete for the single slot
- The swamp lasts only 3 turns and requires both Pledge users alive and moving in the correct order — a fragile plan that collapses if one is KO'd or redirected
- Zapdos ran the wrong item at the event (Power Belt instead of the intended Leftovers), a mistake the author openly documents

### When It Rains, It Pours (Jack Hatch / Logilink)

- **Format:** VGC 2015 (Gen 6 ORAS) — Doubles, bring 6 / pick 4
- **Era:** Gen 6 (ORAS), megas era · **Event:** 1st Place (11-1, undefeated Swiss) — Raleigh Fall Premier Challenge; Top 4 — Cornelius Premier Challenge, 2015 season · **Author:** Jack Hatch (Logilink), USA · **Confidence:** medium
