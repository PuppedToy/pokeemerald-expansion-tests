# Ability-Dependent Combos — Smogon Gen5-9 Research

The most important section: abilities that transformed average Pokemon into tier fixtures.

---

## TIER 1 ABILITIES — Game-Breaking Synergies

### Magic Guard
**What it does:** Prevents all indirect damage (hazards, weather chip, burn, poison, recoil, Leech Seed, Life Orb recoil).
**Pokemon:** Reuniclus (BST 490), Clefable (BST 483), Alakazam (BST 490 / Mega 590)

**Key synergies:**
- Magic Guard + Life Orb = strongest offensive item with ZERO drawback
- Magic Guard + Toxic Orb = immune to poison damage, Toxic Orb does nothing
- Magic Guard + Stealth Rock immunity = freely switches in and out with no chip
- Magic Guard + Calm Mind = setup sweeper that can never be worn down by passive damage

**Why it's broken:** The game balance assumes Life Orb costs 10% HP per attack. Magic Guard removes that assumption entirely, giving effectively a 30% power boost on every move for free.

---

### Poison Heal
**What it does:** Heals 1/8 HP per turn when poisoned (instead of taking damage).
**Pokemon:** Gliscor (BST 510), Breloom (BST 410)

**Key synergies:**
- Poison Heal + Toxic Orb = 12.5% HP per turn passive healing (better than Leftovers)
- Poison Heal + Substitute = Sub costs 25% HP, two turns of PH recovery covers it entirely
- Poison Heal + low Spe = at 25% HP behind Sub, opponent can't break through before PH restores
- Breloom: PH + Spore = put threat to sleep, set up behind Sub

**Why it's broken:** Makes otherwise fragile Pokemon nearly unkillable through attrition. Gliscor with PH+Sub can stall out entire teams with just Toxic + EQ.

---

### Speed Boost
**What it does:** Raises Speed by 1 stage at end of each turn.
**Pokemon:** Blaziken (BST 530), Scolipede (BST 485), Yanmega (BST 515), Sharpedo (BST 460)

**Key synergies:**
- Speed Boost + Protect = waste opponent's turn while gaining +1 Spe for free
- Speed Boost + Swords Dance = one SD + two SB turns → +2 Atk AND +2 Spe simultaneously
- Speed Boost + Baton Pass = transfer accumulated speed to a sweeper (Scolipede)
- Speed Boost + Substitute = behind sub, accumulates speed with no risk

**Why it's broken:** Speed is the stat that determines battle order. Automatically gaining speed removes the need to invest EVs in speed, freeing them for bulk/offense. After 2-3 turns, no Choice Scarf can revenge kill.

**Blaziken banned because:** After SD + 2 SBs: +2 Atk, outspeeds everything. HJK deals 300+ BP equivalent with STAB. Nothing in the game can switch in reliably.

---

### Huge Power / Pure Power
**What it does:** Doubles the Attack stat.
**Pokemon:** Azumarill (BST 420), Mega Mawile (BST 480), Mega Medicham (BST 510)

**Key synergies:**
- Huge Power + Belly Drum = +6 Atk starting from double Attack → absurd numbers
- Huge Power + Aqua Jet = STAB priority that hits as hard as a +2 boosted attack from normal mons
- Pure Power + High Jump Kick = STAB Fighting at effectively 200 base Atk with STAB
- Huge Power + Choice Band = effectively Choice Band on a Pokemon with 100 base Atk

**Why it's broken:** Every physical move is effectively twice as strong. Azumarill at BST 420 hits harder than Mewtwo because of Huge Power + Belly Drum.

---

### Protean / Libero
**What it does:** Changes Pokemon's type to match the move being used → STAB on every move.
**Pokemon:** Greninja (BST 530), Cinderace (BST 530)

**Key synergies:**
- Protean + mixed attacking coverage = every single move gets 1.5x STAB
- Protean + coverage moves = 3-4 different STAB types in one set
- Protean + U-turn = becomes Bug type before the switch, resists differently

**Why it's broken:** Normal STAB incentive is 1.5x on 1-2 types. Protean applies 1.5x to all 4 moves. This is equivalent to a permanent ~30% damage bonus on every attack, plus type-changing tricks opponents' type-based calculations.

---

### Serene Grace
**What it does:** Doubles secondary effect chances.
**Pokemon:** Togekiss (BST 545), Jirachi (BST 600), Blissey (BST 540)

**Key synergies:**
- Serene Grace + Air Slash = 60% flinch rate (was 30%)
- Serene Grace + Iron Head = 60% flinch rate
- Serene Grace + Scald = 60% burn chance
- Serene Grace + Thunder/Ice Beam = 60% paralysis/freeze chance
- Serene Grace + Hustle = Jirachi Iron Head at high Atk with 60% flinch = "paraflinch" strategy

**Why it's broken:** Flinch prevents opponent from moving entirely. 60% flinch on a faster Pokemon means ~60% chance opponent literally cannot attack each turn. After a few turns, the odds compound into near-certainty of inaction.

---

### Technician
**What it does:** Boosts moves with ≤60 BP by 1.5x.
**Pokemon:** Scizor (BST 430), Breloom (BST 410), Mega Scizor (BST 530), Ambipom (BST 482)

**Key synergies:**
- Technician + Bullet Punch = 40 BP → 60 BP (×1.5) + STAB = 90 effective BP priority
- Technician + Mach Punch = same on Breloom
- Technician + Bullet Seed = 25 BP×5 hits → 37.5 BP×5 = 187.5 total + STAB
- Technician + U-turn = 70 BP → not boosted (above 60). Wait — U-turn is 70 BP, so not boosted.
- Technician + Aerial Ace = 60 BP → 90 BP (never misses)
- Technician + False Swipe / Low Sweep = boosted utility moves

**Why it's broken:** Priority moves are balanced by being weak. Technician removes that balance — Bullet Punch becomes as strong as a neutral hit from a non-priority move, while still having +1 priority.

---

### Mold Breaker
**What it does:** Ignores all opponent abilities when attacking.
**Pokemon:** Haxorus (BST 540), Excadrill (BST 508), Mega Ampharos, Pinsir

**Key synergies:**
- Mold Breaker + Earthquake = hits Rotom (Levitate), Bronzong (Levitate), all Levitate users
- Mold Breaker + Dragon Dance = setup sweeper that can't be walled by ability-reliant mons
- Mold Breaker + Stealth Rock (Excadrill) = sets SR through Magic Bounce

**Why it's broken:** Levitate is the most common defensive ability (Rotom-W, Hydreigon, Gengar, Bronzong all use it). Mold Breaker ignores it, closing the "Ground immune" defensive gap completely.

---

### Unburden
**What it does:** Doubles Speed when held item is consumed or knocked off.
**Pokemon:** Hawlucha (BST 500), Accelgor (BST 495), Drifblim (BST 370)

**Key synergies:**
- Unburden + Power Herb = use Power Herb for instant Sky Attack → Unburden activates → 236 effective Spe
- Unburden + Flying Gem + Acrobatics = Flying Gem used, Acrobatics doubles BP, Unburden doubles Spe
- Unburden + Salac/Petaya/Liechi Berry = consumed at low HP → Unburden triggers
- Unburden + Swords Dance = +2 Atk AND double Spe simultaneously

**Why it's broken:** Goes from average speed to outrunning Choice Scarf users in one turn. No Speed investment needed — frees all EVs for bulk/attack.

---

### Wonder Guard
**What it does:** Only super-effective moves deal damage.
**Pokemon:** Shedinja (BST 236, but 1 HP)

**Key synergies:**
- Wonder Guard = immune to 12 of 18 types when Bug/Ghost
- Only Fire, Rock, Flying, Ghost, Dark hit it
- Pattern: cannot be touched by 2/3 of the type chart

**Why it's broken (and why Shedinja is still bad):** 1 HP means one hazard, one weather, one status = instant death. Wonder Guard is the strongest ability conceptually but offset by the worst defensive stats possible.

## TIER 2 ABILITIES — Strong but Contextual

### Intimidate
**What it does:** Lowers opponent's Attack by 1 on switch-in.
**Pokemon:** Landorus-T (BST 580), Gyarados (BST 540), Mega Gyarados, Arcanine (BST 555), Salamence

**Key synergies:**
- Intimidate + U-turn = lower Attack, pivot away, lower Attack again on next switch-in
- Intimidate + physical bulk = can switch into physical attackers multiple times
- Intimidate + Defog/SR = utility Pokemon that also cripples physical threats

---

### Regenerator
**What it does:** Restores 1/3 HP when switching out.
**Pokemon:** Tornadus-T (BST 580), Slowbro/Slowking (BST 490), Tangrowth (BST 535), Toxapex (BST 364), Amoonguss (BST 464)

**Key synergies:**
- Regenerator + pivot move (U-turn/Volt Switch) = net HP positive every switch cycle
- Regenerator + Rocky Helmet = punish contact moves AND heal on retreat
- Regenerator + Knock Off = remove opponent's item AND heal on switch
- Regenerator + bulk = effectively 4/3 HP bar in extended games

---

### Gale Wings (pre-nerf Gen 6)
**What it does:** Gives +1 priority to Flying-type moves (when at full HP in later gens).
**Pokemon:** Talonflame (BST 499)

**Key synergies (Gen 6, pre-nerf — always priority):**
- Gale Wings + Brave Bird = 120 BP STAB at +1 priority (same priority as Extreme Speed)
- Gale Wings + Acrobatics = 110 BP (no item) priority
- Gale Wings + Tailwind = priority speed buff for team
- Gale Wings + Will-O-Wisp = priority burn on switch-in

**Why it was broken (Gen 6):** Brave Bird at +1 priority is stronger than most moves AND has the priority tier of ExtremeSpeed. Only Steel/Rock/Electric could switch in. Was nerfed to "only at full HP" in Gen 7.

---

### Stance Change
**What it does:** Aegislash switches forms when using attack vs King's Shield.
**Pokemon:** Aegislash (BST 520)

**Key synergies:**
- Shield Form (150/150 Def/SpDef) → Attack Form (150/150 Atk/SpAtk) on attacking turn
- King's Shield + Shadow Sneak = forces opponent to attack Shield form → blocks → priority sneak
- Stance Change + Swords Dance = set up in Shield form, sweep in Blade form
- King's Shield punishes physical attackers by halving their Attack if they make contact during it

**Why it's broken:** Has the bulk of a wall AND the offense of a sweeper simultaneously. Forces opponent to account for both 150/150 offense AND 150/150 defense on the same Pokemon.

---

### Unaware
**What it does:** Ignores opponent's stat changes when calculating damage (both offensive and defensive).
**Pokemon:** Clefable (BST 483), Quagsire (BST 430), Clodsire (BST 430), Dondozo (BST 530)

**Key synergies:**
- Unaware + Recover = hard stop to every setup sweeper
- Unaware + Scald = burns even +6 physical attackers (Unaware ignores the boost on defense calc)
- Unaware + Calm Mind = sets up CM while ignoring opponent's CM (Quagsire used this)

**Why it's broken:** The entire offensive metagame revolves around setup moves. Unaware makes all setup completely irrelevant against the user.

---

### Magic Bounce
**What it does:** Reflects all non-damaging moves back at the user.
**Pokemon:** Espeon (BST 525), Mega Absol (BST 565), Xatu (BST 470)

**Key synergies:**
- Magic Bounce + dual screens = can't have screens Taunted away, hazards reflected
- Magic Bounce + Stealth Rock = opponent's SR attempt goes back to them
- Magic Bounce + Taunt = opponent's Taunt reflects back (they Taunt themselves)

**Why it's good:** Hazards are so dominant in competitive play that reflecting them with zero effort is a huge tempo advantage.

---

### Contrary
**What it does:** Inverts stat changes (lowering effects become boosts, boosts become drops).
**Pokemon:** Serperior (BST 528), Malamar (BST 482), Inkay (BST 342)

**Key synergies:**
- Contrary + Leaf Storm = Leaf Storm normally drops SpA by 2, but on Contrary raises SpA by 2
- Contrary + Superpower = normally drops Atk/Def, with Contrary raises them
- Contrary + Overheat = raises SpA by 2 instead of dropping

**Why it's broken:** Leaf Storm is a 130 BP STAB move. Normally it's a desperation nuke because of the self-drop. Contrary Serperior can spam it indefinitely while getting STRONGER each use. After 3 Leaf Storms: +6 SpA.

---

### Beast Boost
**What it does:** Raises highest stat by 1 when KOing an opponent.
**Pokemon:** Ultra Beasts — Kartana (BST 570), Pheromosa (BST 570), Xurkitree (BST 570)

**Key synergies (Kartana, BST 570):**
- Beast Boost + Leaf Blade/Smart Strike/Sacred Sword = boosts highest stat (Atk 181) on KO
- Each kill: +1 Atk from already-180 base
- Pattern: Beast Boost + fast revenge killer = snowball cleaner

---

### Grim Neigh / Chilling Neigh
**What it does:** Raises SpA/Atk by 1 when KOing an opponent.
**Pokemon:** Spectrier (BST 460, SS Uber), Glastrier (BST 590)

**Key synergies (Spectrier):**
- Grim Neigh + Shadow Ball + Nasty Plot = after NP, each KO gives another +1 SpA
- Shadow Ball is 80 BP Ghost with no immunities except Normal/Dark
- BST 460 but in Uber because each kill makes it stronger

---

### Supreme Overlord
**What it does:** Raises Attack 10% for each fainted teammate (max 50%).
**Pokemon:** Kingambit (BST 550)

Already covered in Setup Sweepers section. Key insight: late-game sweeper gets exponentially better as your team gets worn down.

---

### Good as Gold
**What it does:** Immune to all status moves.
**Pokemon:** Gholdengo (BST 550)

**Key synergies:**
- Good as Gold + Nasty Plot = cannot be Taunted, Thunder Waved, or stopped from setting up
- Good as Gold + Shadow Ball/Make It Rain = Steel/Ghost coverage with NP boosts
- Good as Gold = spinblocks (Ghost type, immune to Rapid Spin forcing opponent away)

---

### Vessel of Ruin / Tablets of Ruin / Sword of Ruin / Beads of Ruin
**What it does:** Passively lowers a stat of ALL other Pokemon by 25%.
**Pokemon:** Ting-Lu (BST 570), Wo-Chien (BST 430), Chien-Pao (BST 570), Chi-Yu (BST 480)

**Key synergies (Ting-Lu — Vessel of Ruin, lowers SpA of all others):**
- Vessel of Ruin = passive 25% reduction to every opponent's SpA just by being on the field
- Stealth Rock + Ruination (halves HP) + Earthquake
- At BST 570 with Ground/Dark typing and max bulk, nearly unkillable

**Chi-Yu (BST 480 — Beads of Ruin, lowers Def of all others):**
- Every physical attacker on your team hits 25% harder because of Chi-Yu's passive
- Nasty Plot + Overheat = insane SpA even from 145 base
