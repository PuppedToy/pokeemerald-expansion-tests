# Hazard Setters & Priority Abusers — Smogon Gen5-9 Research

---

## Hazard Setters with Longevity

### Ferrothorn (BST 489, BW+ OU) — Definitive hazard setter
- **Combo:** Stealth Rock + Spikes + Leech Seed + Power Whip
- **Ability:** Iron Barbs
- **Item:** Rocky Helmet / Leftovers
- **Why:** Sets TWO hazard types. Iron Barbs + Rocky Helmet punishes any contact attack (up to 31% damage on contact). Leech Seed provides recovery. Near-impossible to remove hazards against.
- **Pattern:** Dual hazard setter + passive damage on contact = opponent pays for every interaction.

### Skarmory (BST 465, BW+ OU)
- **Combo:** Stealth Rock + Spikes + Whirlwind + Roost
- **Ability:** Sturdy
- **Item:** Leftovers
- **Why:** 140 physical Defense walls physical attackers, sets hazards, Whirlwind forces switches to rack up chip. Roost recovers. Sturdy guarantees survival of one hit.
- **Pattern:** Physical wall + multi-hazard + phazing = passive win condition in balanced teams.

### Garchomp (BST 600 — over threshold but reference)
- **Combo:** Stealth Rock + Earthquake + Dragon Claw + Swords Dance
- **Ability:** Rough Skin
- **Rough Skin punishes contact attacks (1/8 damage), same principle as Iron Barbs.

### Klefki (BST 470, XY+ OU fringe)
- **Combo:** Prankster + Spikes + Thunder Wave + Swagger + Foul Play
- **Ability:** Prankster
- **Why:** Priority Spikes — sets Spikes before opponent can move. Priority Thunder Wave. Swagger + Foul Play = confuse + punish with their own attack stat.
- **Pattern:** Prankster + Spikes = hazards that can't be prevented by faster leads.

### Smeargle (BST 250, hazard lead)
- **Combo:** Spore + Stealth Rock + Spikes + Sticky Web
- **Ability:** Technician / Own Tempo
- **Why:** Spore is 100% accurate sleep. After putting lead to sleep, sets any combination of hazards. Only possible hazard setter for Sticky Web + SR + Spikes in one slot.
- **Pattern:** Spore + hazard stacking = one Pokemon sets entire hazard suite after forcing one sleep.

## Hazard Removal

### Rapid Spin users:
- **Excadrill (BST 508):** Rapid Spin + Sand Rush + Stealth Rock — removes hazards AND sets own SR AND threatens with speed
- **Starmie (BST 520):** Rapid Spin + Natural Cure (clears status on switch) + Recover + Thunderbolt — fast spinner with recovery
- **Tentacruel (BST 515):** Rapid Spin + Toxic Spikes + Scald + Acid Spray — removes AND sets hazards

### Defog users:
- **Corviknight (BST 495):** Defog + Mirror Armor + Roost — clears hazards, reflects Intimidate
- **Landorus-T (BST 580):** Defog variant instead of SR
- **Zapdos (BST 580):** Defog + Discharge + Roost + Heat Wave

### Magic Bounce (passive hazard denial):
- **Espeon (BST 525):** Reflects SR attempts back
- **Mega Absol (BST 565):** Same
- **Pattern:** Magic Bounce = opponent's hazard attempt is punished with hazards on their own side.

## Priority Abusers

### Extreme Speed users
- **Dragonite (BST 600, slightly over):** ExtremeSpeed after DD revenge-kills faster threats
- **Lucario (BST 525):** SD + ExtremeSpeed — see setup section
- **Arcanine (BST 555):** ExtremeSpeed + Extreme Speed + Flare Blitz — natural priority access

### Bullet Punch / Mach Punch (Technician boosted)
- **Scizor (BST 430):** Technician Bullet Punch = 90 effective BP priority — strongest priority in game
- **Breloom (BST 410):** Technician Mach Punch = 60 effective BP + Fighting coverage

### Shadow Sneak
- **Aegislash (BST 520):** King's Shield + Shadow Sneak — forces opponent into Shield form, then priority attacks. 80 BP ghost priority after Stance Change boosts.
- **Mimikyu (BST 476):** Swords Dance + Shadow Sneak — after SD, +2 priority ghost sneak

### Aqua Jet
- **Azumarill (BST 420):** Belly Drum + Aqua Jet — covered in setup section. Water priority after BD.
- **Crawdaunt (BST 468):** Adaptability + Aqua Jet + Crabhammer + Knock Off. Adaptability doubles STAB multiplier to 2x. Aqua Jet hits hard even without setup.

### Sucker Punch
- **Bisharp/Kingambit (BST 490/550):** Sucker Punch + Defiant — opponent can't lower stats safely, priority Dark covers Psychic and Ghost types
- **Pattern:** Sucker Punch works only when opponent uses a damaging move — combined with Defiant (punishes stat drops) creates mind games

### Grassy Glide (terrain priority)
- **Rillaboom (BST 530):** Grassy Surge → Grassy Glide = +1 priority Grass in own terrain
- **Pattern:** Self-set terrain + terrain-boosted priority = priority that doesn't need to be a "weak" move.

### Fake Out
- **Mega Lopunny (BST 528):** Scrappy + Fake Out + High Jump Kick + Return/Frustration
- **Ability:** Scrappy makes Normal/Fighting moves hit Ghost types
- **Why:** Fake Out guaranteed flinch on first turn + HJK with Scrappy = hits Ghost types that normally wall Fighting. Fake Out buys a free turn.
- **Pattern:** Fake Out + Scrappy = turn 1 flinch then STAB Fighting that hits every type.

## Baton Pass Chains

### Scolipede (BST 485, BW/XY OU — then banned from Baton Pass)
- **Combo:** Speed Boost + Baton Pass + Swords Dance + Substitute
- **Ability:** Speed Boost
- **Why:** Fastest Baton Pass user. After 2 turns: +2 Spe, can also pass Substitute and SD boosts to sweeper. Entire team benefits.

### Espeon (BST 525, BW/XY OU)
- **Combo:** Magic Bounce + Baton Pass + Stored Power + Calm Mind
- **Why:** Magic Bounce prevents hazards on BP chains. Stored Power scales with accumulated stat boosts (base 20 BP + 20 per boost). After chain: Stored Power can hit 820 BP.

### Ninjask (BST 456, older gens)
- **Combo:** Speed Boost + Swords Dance + Baton Pass + Substitute
- **Why:** Original BP user — passes +Speed and +Attack to recipient.

### Sylveon (BST 525, XY+ OU) — Cleric / Wish passer
- **Combo:** Wish + Protect + Hyper Voice + Calm Mind / Heal Bell
- **Ability:** Pixilate (Hyper Voice becomes Fairy-type STAB at 1.2x boost → 108 effective BP)
- **Item:** Leftovers
- **Why:** Wish passes HP to teammates. Pixilate-boosted Hyper Voice hits both mons in doubles and is unblocked by Substitute. Heal Bell cures team status. Cleric role with offensive presence.
- **Pattern:** Pixilate + Hyper Voice = spread STAB that bypasses Substitute.
